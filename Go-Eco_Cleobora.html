Cleobora Mellyi the Southern Ladybird

Cleobora mellyi, the Southern ladybird (Farm Forestry website)

Cleobora mellyi is a predator of psyllids and Tortoise beetle (leaf beetle) eggs in its native Australia. It was introduced into New Zealand as a predator of eucalypt pests. I have devised an efficient method of mass rearing Cleobora and other coccinellids using artificial diets. A larval powder diet and an adult wet diet are available. There is considerable potential for utilising mass releases of coccinellids for  controlling forestry and horticultural pests, such as the tomato/potato psyllid (Bactericera cockerelli) in New Zealand and the Chrysomelid beetles (Chrysophtharta bimaculata and C. agricola)  in Tasmania.
