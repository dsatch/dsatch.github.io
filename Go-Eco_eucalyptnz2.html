Eucalypt species selection for New Zealand's environment requires an extensive understanding of the dynamics and interaction of microclimate, pests/diseases and desired timber properties.
  • Some eucalyptus species are highly susceptable to the suite of pests and disease arrivals from Australia, but others are highly resistant.
  • Eucalyptus species vary in their resistence to drought, soil conditions, wind snow and frost.
With this in mind I am preparing an information resource to help with species selection for plantation forestry in New Zealand. Watch this space.
