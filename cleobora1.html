Cleobora mellyi, the Southern ladybird (Farm Forestry website)

Cleobora mellyi is a predator of psyllids and Tortoise beetle (leaf beetle) eggs and young larvae in its native Australia. It was introduced into New Zealand as a predator of eucalypt pests. I have developed an efficient method of mass rearing Cleobora and other coccinellids using artificial diets. There is considerable potential for utilising mass releases of coccinellids for  controlling forestry and horticultural pests, such as the tomato/potato psyllid (Bactericera cockerelli) in New Zealand and the Chrysomelid beetles (Chrysophtharta bimaculata and C. agricola)  in Tasmania.
