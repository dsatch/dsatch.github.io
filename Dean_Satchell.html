Society has a responsibility to ensure environmental sustainability is practiced and continues to be practiced into the future. Increasing productivity by intensification will inevitably come at the expense of the environment. Sustainability for land users is about finding the appropriate tradeoff between productivity and profit that supports both economic and environmental sustainability.

Responsible land management involves a broad understanding of soils, ecology, renewable and finite resources, economics and energy use. With my strong interest in soil science and environmental sustainability, I can advise  on land management options and appropriate land-uses such as plantation forestry and agriculture.

I provide inexpensive, sustainable solutions for land-users in Northland.
